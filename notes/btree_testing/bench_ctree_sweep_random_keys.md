## Key Takeaways

1. **The current default (N=4, P=u64) ranks 24th out of 32** — nearly the worst configuration.
2. **N=6, P=u16 is the composite winner** (2.007x geomean), excelling at forward iteration (3.78x) with solid all-around performance and good memory efficiency (54.9 bytes/key vs 81.8 baseline).
3. **N=16, P=u64 dominates forward iteration** (1.24G, +350%), but loses on lookup and uses more memory.
4. **P=u64 is consistently the worst preview type** for each fanout — the full 8-byte preview is overkill for the SIMD comparison. P=u16 and P=u32 give much better discrimination per byte of preview storage.
5. **Sweet spots by metric**: N=8 P=u32 for lookup (12.19M), N=16 P=u64 for fwd iter (1.24G), N=16 P=u8 for memory (41.0 bytes/key).
6. **u64 preview has a striking fwd-iter regression** — every fanout shows P=u64 significantly slower than P=u16/u32 for forward iteration, likely due to cache pressure from the larger preview array.
